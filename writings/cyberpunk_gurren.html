\documentclass{article} \begin{document} \section*{Personal Motivation} I just finished watching watching Cyberpunk a couple of days ago and as much as I'd love to jump onto the hypetrain and say that it's amazing, I'm not quite sure how I feel about it. The ending sure did hit though. It felt like your typical netflix show nowadays, day, filled with gore and sex, the world is a dog eat dog world and the once naive main character must join in all the carnage or perish. This is very much a generalization of netflix shows, but nevertheless, I want to understand Cyberpunk and love it in all it's g(l)ory. So here I am. While slowly unravelling the tale of Cyberpunk, another series appeared in the edge of my mind and I started to wonder, although the two are very different in tone, at core, I have an inkling Gurren Lagann shares a very similar tale and message. \subsection*{Disclaimer: } The motivation behind this essay isn't to claim that Cyberpunk is a copy of Gurren Lagann, or that one series is better than another, but to instead further enlighten ourselves through holding the two series side by side. Cyberpunk Brain Dump:

  • Cyberpunk is set in a single city dominated by technology. There seems to be clear class distinctions, with a lower and upper class. All the people seem to be able to plug themselves into the city - with a usb c connection. Through these connections, it seems like users are able to experience different environments, experience other POVs, work and communicate.
  • The story revolves around a boy named David Martinez. His mother - a paramedic? - and him live in the lower economic district, where she just barely makes enough to send David to an elite school. David is a stellar student and his mother dreams of him climbing the corporate ladder.
  • It seems like a mark of success in the city is your corporate standing.
